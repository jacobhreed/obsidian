## Definition

Best Bank generates revenue through four streams: net interest income (NII), card interchange, account/service fees, and other income. NII from the deposit base is dominant today; Best Credit is shifting the mix toward interchange and revolving interest income.

## How it works

### 2025 actuals ($236M total)

| Stream | Amount | Share | Driver |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Net interest income | $172.3M | 73% | Spread between deposit rates paid and asset yields; driven by $4.2B deposit base |
| Card interchange | $28.3M | 12% | Debit card swipes on Best Checking accounts |
| Account & service fees | $21.3M | 9% | Monthly fees, wire fees, paper statement fees |
| Other income | $14.1M | 6% | Wires, FX, treasury services |

### 2028 projection ($388M total)

| Stream | Amount | Change | Driver |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Net interest income | $262M | +52% | Deposit growth + Best Credit revolving balances |
| Card interchange | $62M | +119% | Best Credit card spend added on top of debit |
| Account & service fees | $38M | +79% | Active account growth |
| Other income | $26M | +84% | Volume-driven |

### NIM (Net Interest Margin)

NIM is the spread between what the bank earns on assets and pays on deposits. Currently 3.85% (2025), rising to 4.10% (2028). Key sensitivities:
- A 100bps Fed rate drop → NIM compresses ~25bps → ~$9M NII reduction in 2027
- Deposit mix shift from checking to high-APY savings compresses margin; tiered savings pricing is the mitigation
- Best Credit balances add a new, higher-yield asset class ($310M → $980M), improving NIM

### Monthly revenue trajectory (2025)

Revenue grew steadily throughout 2025: $18.2M (Jan) → $21.2M (Dec). No flat or down months. Run rate by year-end implies a natural base of ~$250M+ heading into 2026, before Best Credit contribution.
