## Definition

Best Bank's product strategy is a sequential funnel: acquire customers with a no-friction checking account, deepen the relationship with savings, and extend into credit. Each step increases revenue per customer and lock-in.

## How it works

```
Best Checking (Acquisition)
    ↓ round-ups, linked account
Best Savings (Deepening)
    ↓ known-customer approval lift
Best Credit (Extension)
```

**Stage 1 — Best Checking:** Zero friction, no fees, no minimums. The "Bring a Friend" referral program is the #1 acquisition channel. Checking gives the bank cash-flow visibility into the customer — the foundation for every downstream product.

**Stage 2 — Best Savings:** Introduced in 2013. Deepens relationship via linked round-ups from checking. Increases the customer's deposit base and the bank's NIM-generating assets. Goal Buckets increase stickiness.

**Stage 3 — Best Credit:** Launched Q1 2026. Existing checking/savings customers get a "known customer" approval lift because the bank already sees their cash flow and savings behavior. The 2026 plan projects 12% of checking customers adopt Best Credit within 18 months.

## Why this matters

- The funnel means each product investment also supports the others — improving checking retention is the foundation for credit adoption
- Card interchange from Best Credit is the fastest-growing revenue stream in projections ($28M → $62M by 2028)
- Best Credit interest income from revolving balances ($310M → $980M by 2028) changes the revenue mix fundamentally
- For commercial clients, the funnel is Checking → Savings → Line of Credit
