## Summary

Product documentation for Best Savings, launched 2013. Two products: High-Yield Savings (tiered APY 3.25%–4.10%) and CDs (6–48 month, 3.90%–4.40% APY). As of YE 2025: ~280,000 accounts, $2.0B in balances. The largest single contributor to net interest income.

## Key points

- **High-Yield Savings:** No fee, no minimum. APY 3.25% (<$10K) → 3.75% ($10K–$50K) → 4.10% ($50K+). Up to 6 withdrawals/cycle
- **CDs:** $1K minimum; 6mo (3.90%) / 12mo (4.25%) / 24mo (4.40%) / 48mo (4.15%). 90–180 day early withdrawal penalty. Auto-renews
- Key features: Goal buckets (split account into named sub-goals), Round-ups from Best Checking, full rate transparency in-app
- "Goal buckets" feature was the top customer request — shipped 2024
- Rate risk: a shift from low-cost checking balances to high-APY savings compresses NIM. Treasury uses tiered pricing as mitigation
- Treasury (Priya Raman) sets rates; changes tracked in Lighthouse
- NII from savings is the largest revenue stream in financial projections
- A 100bps rate move materially affects both APYs paid and NIM
