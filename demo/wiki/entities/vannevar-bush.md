## Overview

American engineer and science administrator (1890–1974), director of the Office of Scientific Research and Development during WWII. Best known in knowledge-management contexts for his 1945 essay "As We May Think," in which he described the **Memex**.

## Role in this wiki

Cited as the intellectual ancestor of the LLM Wiki pattern — his vision of personal, associatively-linked knowledge stores anticipates what LLMs can now automate.

## Key facts

- Proposed the **Memex** in 1945: a personal device for storing and retrieving documents with associative linking between them
- Bush's insight was that the value of a knowledge store lies in the *connections* between documents, not just the documents themselves
- The part he couldn't solve: who does the maintenance? LLMs answer that question
